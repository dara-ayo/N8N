# App Walkthrough

**Project:** Content Generation & Publishing Automation
**Builder:** Ayodele Oluwafimidaraayo
**Date:** 2026-03-26

This document walks through every screen and action in the application, from login to publishing.

---

## Step 1: Login

**Screen:** Login Page (`/login`)

1. Open the app at `localhost:3000` in your browser.
2. You see a clean login page with an email input field and a "Send Magic Link" button. There are no password fields.
3. Enter your email address (must be associated with an active team member record in Supabase).
4. Click **Send Magic Link**.
5. A confirmation message appears: "Check your email for a magic link."
6. Open your email inbox and find the email from Supabase Auth with the subject "Your Magic Link."
7. Click the link in the email.
8. You are redirected back to the app and authenticated. The `AuthProvider` picks up the Supabase session, fetches your role from the `team_members` table, and routes you to the Dashboard.

**What can go wrong:**
- If the magic link has expired (default: 1 hour), you see "This link has expired. Please request a new one."
- If your email is not in the `team_members` table, you see "Your account is not associated with any team. Contact your team admin for an invite."
- If your team member status is "deactivated," you see "Your account has been deactivated. Contact your team admin."

---

## Step 2: Dashboard

**Screen:** Dashboard (`/`)

1. After login, you land on the Dashboard. This is the home screen for all users.
2. You see a list of all submissions, displayed as cards or table rows. Each submission shows:
   - **Title or content preview** (first 100 characters of the raw input)
   - **Status badge** color-coded by state:
     - Gray: "Generating" (drafts are being created)
     - Yellow: "Pending Review" (drafts ready for selection)
     - Blue: "Processing" (adaptation in progress)
     - Green: "Published" (content sent to platforms)
     - Red: "Error" (something failed)
   - **Submission date** and **last updated** timestamp
3. The dashboard auto-refreshes every 15 seconds, so you see status changes without manually reloading.
4. Click any submission to go to the Submission Detail page.

**Who sees what:**
- All roles (owner, admin, editor, viewer) can see the Dashboard.
- Viewers see everything but cannot create new submissions or take any actions.

---

## Step 3: Submit New Content

**Screen:** New Submission (`/submit`)

1. Click the **"New Submission"** button in the nav bar or on the Dashboard. (Visible to editors, admins, and owners only.)
2. You see a submission form with:
   - A text area for your content idea (e.g., "Why most startups fail at content marketing and what they should do instead")
   - A URL input field as an alternative (e.g., paste a blog post URL to generate drafts based on that content)
   - A publish mode selector: **Immediate** (publish right after adaptation) or **Manual** (review adapted content before publishing)
3. Enter your content idea or paste a URL. You only need one; if both are provided, the URL takes priority.
4. Click **Submit**.
5. The app sends a POST request to n8n Workflow 1 (`/content-submit`).
6. You see a loading state: "Generating drafts... this takes about 15-20 seconds."
7. Behind the scenes, Workflow 1:
   - Validates the input and checks for duplicates
   - Extracts content from the URL (if provided) or uses the raw idea
   - Saves the submission to Supabase with status "generating"
   - Generates three SEO-optimized drafts sequentially via OpenAI, each from a distinct angle
   - Validates each draft for length and structure
   - Updates the submission in Supabase with all three drafts and status "pending_review"
8. When complete, the submission appears on the Dashboard with a "Pending Review" badge. Click it to review the drafts.

**What can go wrong:**
- Duplicate content (submitted within the last 10 minutes): "This idea was already submitted recently. Check your pending reviews."
- URL that returns no extractable content: "Could not extract content from this URL."
- OpenAI API errors after retries: Status changes to "Error" with details about which draft generation step failed.

## Step 4: Review and Select a Draft

**Screen:** Submission Detail (`/submissions/:id`)

1. Click a submission from the Dashboard to open its detail page.
2. If the status is "Pending Review," you see **three draft cards** arranged side by side (or stacked on mobile). Each card shows:
   - **Angle badge** at the top: "Contrarian," "How-To," or "Data & Trends"
   - **Draft title** generated by the AI
   - **Content preview** (first 300 characters of the article body)
   - **Word count** displayed at the bottom
   - A **"Select This Draft"** button
3. Read through all three drafts. They represent genuinely different editorial angles on the same content idea:
   - **Contrarian** challenges common assumptions about the topic
   - **How-To** turns the idea into actionable, step-by-step guidance
   - **Data & Trends** grounds the content in industry statistics and patterns
4. Click **"Select This Draft"** on the one you want to publish.
5. A confirmation dialog appears: "This will adapt the selected draft for LinkedIn, X, and Newsletter. Continue?"
6. Click **Confirm**.
7. The app sends a POST request to n8n Workflow 2 (`/draft-select`).
8. You see a loading state: "Adapting content for all platforms... this takes about 15 seconds."
9. Behind the scenes, Workflow 2:
   - Validates the selection and locks the submission (status changes to "processing")
   - Adapts the selected draft for LinkedIn using PAS format (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
   - Adapts for X/Twitter with a hard 280-character limit
   - Adapts for email newsletter with a subject line and 250-600 word body
   - Saves all adapted content to the `adapted_content` table in Supabase
10. When complete, the page refreshes to show the adapted content section.

**Who can select:**
- Editors, admins, and owners can select drafts.
- Viewers can see the drafts but the "Select This Draft" button is not shown.

---

## Step 5: Review Adapted Content

**Screen:** Submission Detail (`/submissions/:id`) -- Adapted Content Section

After a draft is selected and adapted, the Submission Detail page shows a new section below the draft cards with three adapted content previews:

### LinkedIn Post
- Displayed in a card styled to resemble a LinkedIn post
- Uses PAS format: Problem statement, Agitation of the problem, Solution with actionable advice
- Character count shown (must be 3000 or fewer)
- Includes a call-to-action at the end
- If truncated, a "Truncated" indicator appears

### X/Twitter Tweet
- Displayed in a card styled to resemble a tweet
- Exactly 280 characters or fewer
- Punchy, self-contained, and designed for engagement
- Character count shown with a visual indicator (green if under 250, yellow if 250-280)

### Email Newsletter
- Displayed in a card resembling an email preview
- Shows the **subject line** at the top (separate from the body)
- Body content is 250-600 words
- Includes a clear call-to-action
- Word count shown at the bottom

All three adapted versions are read-only on this screen. They were generated from the same selected draft but formatted specifically for each platform's requirements and audience behavior.

## Step 6: Approve and Publish (Admin/Owner Only)

**Screen:** Submission Detail (`/submissions/:id`) -- Publish Section

1. Below the adapted content previews, admins and owners see a blue **"Ready to Publish"** card. (Editors and viewers do not see this card.)
2. The card shows:
   - A summary of which platforms are connected (green checkmarks for connected, gray for disconnected)
   - An **"Approve & Publish Now"** button
3. Click **"Approve & Publish Now"**.
4. A confirmation dialog appears: "This will publish the adapted content to all connected platforms. This action cannot be undone."
5. Click **Confirm**.
6. The app sends a POST request to n8n Workflow 4 (`/publish-content`).
7. You see a publishing progress indicator.
8. Behind the scenes, Workflow 4:
   - Fetches the adapted content from Supabase
   - Fetches platform credentials from the `platform_connections` table
   - Checks if any OAuth tokens are expired and refreshes them if needed
   - Posts to the LinkedIn API with the adapted LinkedIn content
   - Posts to the Twitter API with the adapted tweet
   - Sends the newsletter via the Resend API with the subject line and body
   - Updates the submission status and records published timestamps per platform
9. When complete:
   - The status badge changes to **"Published"** (green)
   - A green success banner appears: "Content published to all platforms"
   - Published timestamps appear next to each platform's adapted content

**What can go wrong:**
- Platform not connected: "LinkedIn is not connected. Go to Settings to connect it."
- Token expired and refresh failed: "LinkedIn connection expired. Please reconnect in Settings."
- Platform API error: "Twitter publish failed: [error detail]. LinkedIn and Newsletter published successfully." (Partial success is reported per platform.)

## Step 7: Team Management (Admin/Owner Only)

**Screen:** Team Page (`/team`)

1. Click **"Team"** in the navigation bar. (Only visible to admins and owners. Editors and viewers do not see this nav link.)
2. You see a table of all team members with the following columns:
   - **Email** (or name, if available)
   - **Role badge**: Owner (purple), Admin (blue), Editor (green), Viewer (gray)
   - **Status badge**: Active (green), Invited (yellow), Deactivated (red)
   - **Actions** column with role change and deactivate/reactivate options

### Invite a New Member

1. Click the **"Invite Member"** button above the team table.
2. A modal appears with:
   - Email input field
   - Role dropdown: Admin, Editor, or Viewer (Owner cannot be assigned this way)
3. Enter the new member's email and select their role.
4. Click **"Send Invite"**.
5. The system:
   - Creates a `team_members` row with status "invited" and a unique `invite_token`
   - Generates an invite link in the format: `{app-url}/invite/{token}`
6. You see the invite link displayed — click to copy it or the system shows a confirmation.
7. Share the link with the new member via your preferred communication channel.
8. When the recipient clicks the invite link:
   - They land on the **AcceptInvite** page (`/invite/:token`)
   - The page looks up the token and displays their pending role (e.g., "You've been invited as Editor")
   - They enter their email address and click **"Accept Invite"**
   - A Supabase magic link is sent to their email
   - When they click the magic link, the `onAuthStateChange` listener fires and calls `acceptInvite()`, which links their authenticated user ID to the `team_members` record
   - Their status changes from "invited" to "active" and they are redirected to the Dashboard
   - They can now access the app with their assigned role

**Error states on the AcceptInvite page:**
- Invalid or expired token: "This invitation link is invalid or has expired."
- Already accepted (status=active): Redirected directly to dashboard
- Revoked (status=deactivated): "This invitation has been revoked. Please contact your team administrator."

### Resend an Invite

If a pending member has not accepted their invite:
1. In the team table, their status badge shows "Invited" (yellow).
2. Click the **"Resend Invite"** button in their row's Actions column.
3. The invite link is copied to your clipboard with a confirmation alert.
4. Share the link again with the member.

### Change a Member's Role

1. In the team table, click the role dropdown next to a member.
2. Select the new role.
3. The change takes effect immediately. The member's permissions update on their next action or page navigation.

**Restrictions:**
- The owner's role cannot be changed by anyone (the dropdown is disabled for the owner row).
- You cannot change your own role (self-demotion prevention).
- Editors and viewers cannot change anyone's role (the actions column is not shown for these roles).

### Deactivate a Member

1. Click the **"Deactivate"** button next to a member.
2. Confirm the action.
3. The member's status changes to "deactivated" and their status badge turns red.
4. The deactivated member can no longer access the app. Their next action or page load will redirect them to the login page with a "Your account has been deactivated" message.
5. To reactivate, click **"Reactivate"** next to the deactivated member.

## Step 8: Platform Settings (Admin/Owner Only)

**Screen:** Settings Page (`/settings`)

1. Click **"Settings"** in the navigation bar. (Only visible to admins and owners.)
2. You see three platform cards arranged in a grid:

### LinkedIn Card
- **Status indicator**: Green "Connected" badge (with account name) or gray "Not Connected"
- **Connect button**: Click to start the LinkedIn OAuth 2.0 flow
  1. You are redirected to LinkedIn's authorization page
  2. Log in to LinkedIn (if not already) and authorize the app
  3. LinkedIn redirects back to the app
  4. n8n Workflow 3 exchanges the authorization code for access and refresh tokens
  5. Tokens are stored in the `platform_connections` table
  6. The Settings page shows "Connected" with your LinkedIn account name
- **Disconnect button** (shown when connected): Removes the stored credentials

### X/Twitter Card
- **Status indicator**: Green "Connected" badge (with @handle) or gray "Not Connected"
- **Connect button**: Click to start the Twitter OAuth 2.0 + PKCE flow
  1. The frontend generates a PKCE code_verifier and code_challenge
  2. You are redirected to Twitter's authorization page
  3. Log in to Twitter and authorize the app
  4. Twitter redirects back to the app with an authorization code
  5. The Operations workflow exchanges the code + code_verifier for access and refresh tokens
  6. Tokens are stored in the `platform_connections` table
  7. The Settings page shows "Connected" with your @handle
- **Disconnect button** (shown when connected): Removes the stored credentials

**Note on current status:** The Twitter OAuth flow is fully built and functional. The connection and token storage work correctly. However, publishing to Twitter is currently blocked because the developer account's free API tier credits are depleted. Publish attempts return a 429 from Twitter's API. This is an account billing issue, not a code issue — publishing will work once credits are restored.

### Newsletter Card
- **Status indicator**: Green "Connected" badge (with ESP name) or gray "Not Connected"
- **Configure button**: Click to open the newsletter configuration form
  1. Select your ESP provider: Resend, SendGrid, or Mailchimp
  2. Enter your API key
  3. Enter the sender email address (must match a verified domain on your ESP)
  4. Enter the sender name
  5. Click **Save**
  6. The system validates the API key by making a test call to the ESP
  7. If valid, credentials are saved to `platform_connections` and the card shows "Connected"
- **Update / Disconnect options** (shown when connected)

**Security note:** The Settings page reads from the `platform_connections_safe` database view, which masks all tokens with "--------". Raw tokens are never sent to the frontend. Only n8n Workflow 4 reads the actual tokens when publishing.

## Status Flow Reference

A submission moves through these statuses during its lifecycle:

```
[Submit] -----> generating -----> pending_review -----> processing -----> published
                    |                                       |                |
                    v                                       v                v
                  error                                   error        partially_published
```

| Status | Meaning | Next Action |
|---|---|---|
| generating | Workflow 1 is creating 3 drafts | Wait 15-20 seconds |
| pending_review | Drafts are ready | Select a draft |
| processing | Workflow 2 is adapting content | Wait 15 seconds |
| adapted | Content is ready for all platforms | Review and approve |
| published | Content sent to all platforms | Done |
| partially_published | Some platforms succeeded, some failed | Retry failed platforms |
| error | Something went wrong | Check error details, resubmit or retry |
